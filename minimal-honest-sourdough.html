Ingredients & Water

  • Flour: Whole‑grain rye or whole‑wheat helps kickstart a robust starter (more natural microbes & enzymes). Switch to bread flour for baking if you want a lighter crumb.
  • Water: Use filtered or de‑chlorinated water. If tap is chlorinated, leave it uncovered overnight or boil then cool.
  • Salt (for dough only): 2% of flour weight (e.g., 8 g per 400 g flour) for flavor and gluten strength.

We use a 1:1 flour:water ratio by weight (aka 100% hydration) for the starter. For a reliable loaf: 70%–75% hydration dough is friendly to handle.

Utensils & Why They Matter

  • Digital scale: Precision matters more than any single “trick.”
  • Glass jar (smooth‑sided) + breathable cover: Lets CO₂ escape; easy to see rise & bubbles. Avoid air‑tight seals during active fermentation.
  • Sturdy mixing bowl & spatula/spoon: For clean mixing; a bench scraper helps handle sticky dough.
  • Banneton or bowl lined with a floured tea towel: Supports shape during final proof.
  • Razor/lame: Score to control expansion (oven spring).
  • Heavy Dutch oven with tight lid (22–26 cm): Traps steam for glossy crust. Preheat it.
  • Oven mitts + safe surface: Cast iron is extremely hot—work deliberately.
Why lid on/off? The lid traps steam for the first phase, keeping the crust flexible so the loaf can expand. Removing the lid lets the crust dry and brown deeply.

Make a Wild Starter (Days 1–7)

  1. Day 1: In a clean jar, mix 50 g whole‑grain flour + 50 g water (room‑temp, non‑chlorinated). Stir thoroughly. Cover loosely. Keep at 23–26 °C.
  2. Days 2–3: Every 24 h (or 12 h if warm), discard about half, then feed with equal weights flour+water (e.g., 50 g + 50 g). Expect small bubbles and a mild tangy aroma.
  3. Days 4–7: Continue discard/feed. Starter should double in 6–12 h after a feeding. When it consistently doubles and smells pleasantly tangy, it’s ready.
  4. Readiness checks: Bubbly, domed surface; webby interior; passes “float test” (a spoonful floats in room‑temp water). If it sinks but looks active, give it 1–2 more vigorous feeds.
Safety: If you see colored mold, fuzzy growth, or smell anything rotten/putrid (not just sour), discard the starter, wash with hot soapy water, and begin again with clean tools.

Starter Aromas: What “Good” Smells Like

  • Healthy: pleasantly sour, yogurt‑like, fruity, or mildly “boozy.”
  • Needs food: more acidic/vinegary, solvent‑like notes — feed and it should sweeten.
  • Unhealthy (discard): rotten, putrid, musty, or visible mold of any color.

Dough: Mix → Bulk → Shape → Proof

  1. Leaven (optional but helpful for timing): 1–2 tbsp ripe starter + 50 g flour + 50 g water. Ferment until doubled (3–6 h at 23–26 °C).
  2. Mix dough (example loaf):
    Flour 400 g (bread or mix of bread + 10–20% whole‑grain)
    Water 280–300 g (70–75% hydration)
    Salt 8 g (2%)
    Ripe starter 80–100 g (20–25% of flour weight)
    Mix to shaggy. Rest 20–30 min (autolyse). Add salt (if held back) and knead lightly or perform 3–4 stretch‑and‑fold sets every 20–30 min.
  3. Bulk ferment: 3–6 h at 23–26 °C until dough is ~50% larger, jiggly, and aerated. Avoid over‑proofing (dough becomes weak/collapses).
  4. Pre‑shape → Rest 15–20 min → Final shape. Place seam‑side up in a generously floured banneton/tea‑towel‑lined bowl.
  5. Cold proof: Cover and refrigerate 8–16 h at 3–5 °C for deeper flavor and easier scoring.

Bake: Dutch Oven & Oven Modes

  1. Preheat: Place Dutch oven with lid inside the oven. Preheat to 250 °C for at least 30–45 min.
  2. Load & steam phase: Invert cold‑proofed dough onto parchment. Score. Carefully lower into the hot Dutch oven. Lid on, bake 20 minutes.
  3. Dry & color phase: Reduce to 230 °C. Remove the lid and bake 20–25 minutes, until crust is deep golden and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped. Internal temp target: ~96–99 °C.
  4. Conventional vs fan‑forced (convection): Both work. Convection runs hotter/more efficient; if using fan‑forced, consider reducing set temp by ~10–15 °C or watch for faster browning. The closed Dutch oven moderates airflow differences.
  5. Cool completely: Let cool on a rack at least 90 minutes before slicing to set the crumb.
Crust & crumb tips: For thinner crust, vent the oven door slightly for the last 5 minutes. For more open crumb, avoid adding extra flour during shaping and proof fully but not past collapse.

Starter Storage & Maintenance

  • Frequent baking (counter‑kept): Keep at room temp and feed daily (e.g., 1:1:1 by weight starter:flour:water). Aim for peak activity to bake.
  • Occasional baking (fridge): Store covered in the refrigerator. Feed weekly. Before baking, give 1–2 room‑temp feeds spaced 8–12 h apart until it doubles reliably.
  • Best place to keep it: A stable‑temperature spot away from direct sunlight; 23–26 °C is the sweet zone.
  • Travel/backup: Dry some starter thinly on parchment; once brittle, crumble into a jar. To revive, soak and feed over 2–3 days.
