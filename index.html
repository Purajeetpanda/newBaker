Ingredients & Water

ItemDetails
FlourWhole-grain rye or whole-wheat jumpstarts fermentation (more native microbes & enzymes). For lighter crumb, use strong bread flour in the final dough.
WaterUse filtered/de-chlorinated water. If tap is chlorinated, leave uncovered overnight or boil then cool.
Salt (for dough)~2% of flour weight (e.g., 8 g per 400 g flour) for flavor and gluten strength.

Starter hydration: 100% (equal weights flour:water). Example dough: 70–75% hydration for easy handling.

Utensils & Why They Matter

  • Digital scale: Accuracy beats guesswork.
  • Glass jar + breathable cover: Track rise/bubbles; avoid airtight lids during active fermentation.
  • Mixing bowl + spatula/bench scraper: Clean mixing and handling.
  • Banneton or bowl + floured tea towel: Supports shape during final proof.
  • Razor/lame: Scoring controls expansion (oven spring).
  • Heavy Dutch oven with tight lid (22–26 cm): Traps steam for a glossy crust—preheat it.
  • Oven mitts + safe surface: Cast iron is extremely hot—work deliberately.
Lid on/off: Lid traps steam early so the crust stays elastic and the loaf expands. Removing the lid later dries and browns the crust.

Make a Wild Starter (Days 1–7)

  1. Day 1: In a clean jar, mix 50 g whole-grain flour + 50 g water (room temp). Cover loosely. Keep at 23–26 °C.
  2. Days 2–3: Every 24 h (or 12 h if warm), discard ~half, then feed equal weights flour+water (e.g., 50 g + 50 g). Expect small bubbles and mild tang.
  3. Days 4–7: Continue feedings. Starter should double in 6–12 h after a feed. When it consistently doubles and smells pleasant, it’s ready.
  4. Readiness checks: Bubbly, domed surface; webby interior; float test may help (a spoonful floats in room-temp water). If it sinks but looks active, give 1–2 robust feeds.
Safety: If you see colored mold or smell anything rotten/putrid (not just sour), discard the starter, wash with hot soapy water, and start again with clean tools.

Starter Aromas: What “Good” Smells Like

  • Healthy: pleasantly sour, yogurt-like, fruity, or mildly “boozy.”
  • Needs food: sharper/vinegary or solvent-like — feed and it should sweeten within a cycle.
  • Unhealthy (discard): rotten, putrid, musty, or visible mold of any color.

Storage: Once mature, you can keep the starter at room temp with daily feeds, or store in the fridge and feed 1×/week. Use it soon after it peaks for best lift.

Dough: Mix → Bulk → Shape → Proof

  1. Build leaven (optional, for timing): 1–2 tbsp ripe starter + 50 g flour + 50 g water. Ferment until doubled (3–6 h @ 23–26 °C).
  2. Mix dough (example):
    ComponentWeight
    Flour (bread; +10–20% whole-grain optional)400 g
    Water280–300 g (70–75% hydration)
    Salt8 g (2%)
    Ripe starter80–100 g (20–25% of flour weight)
    Mix to shaggy. Rest 20–30 min (autolyse). Knead briefly or perform 3–4 stretch-and-fold sets every 20–30 min.
  3. Bulk ferment: 3–6 h @ 23–26 °C until ~50% larger, jiggly, and aerated. Avoid over-proofing (dough weak/deflates).
  4. Pre-shape → Rest 15–20 min → Final shape. Place seam-side up in a well-floured banneton/tea-towel-lined bowl.
  5. Cold proof: Cover and refrigerate 8–16 h @ 3–5 °C for deeper flavor and easier scoring.

Bake: Dutch Oven & Oven Modes

  1. Preheat: Place Dutch oven with lid inside. Preheat to 250 °C for 30–45 min.
  2. Load & steam phase: Invert cold-proofed dough onto parchment. Score. Carefully lower into hot Dutch oven. Lid on → bake 20 min.
  3. Dry & color: Reduce to 230 °C. Remove lid, bake 20–25 min more until deep golden and hollow-sounding. Internal temp ~96–99 °C.
  4. Conventional vs fan-forced (convection): Convection browns faster; reduce set temp by ~10–15 °C or monitor closely. A closed Dutch oven moderates airflow differences.
  5. Cool completely: Rest on a rack ≥ 90 min before slicing.
Crust & crumb: For thinner crust, vent the oven door slightly for the last 5 min. For a more open crumb, avoid excess flour during shaping and proof fully—but not past collapse.

Troubleshooting & Safety

Common Issues

  • Dense loaf: Starter under-ripe; bulk too short; dough too cold. Fix: warm environment (23–26 °C) and proof longer.
  • Spreading loaf: Over-proofed or under-developed gluten. Fix: shorten bulk or add folds; ensure proper shaping tension.
  • Sour/bitter: Over-fermented. Fix: feed starter more frequently; reduce cold proof time.

Food Safety

  • Use clean jars/tools; wash hands. Discard at any sign of mold or putrid odor.
  • Refrigerate starter when not baking often; feed weekly.
  • Ensure loaf reaches ~96–99 °C internal temperature for doneness.
